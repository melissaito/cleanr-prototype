the problem

Lint can wreck your septic system. And your wallet.

Septic systems are designed to break down waste, not handle the constant flow of hair and lint from modern laundry. Over time, that buildup reduces flow, clogs your system, and leads to serious (and expensive) issues.

1

Hair and lint slip past the tank and clog the drain field over time. When the field plugs, wastewater can’t soak in — often requiring a full excavation and replacement.

2

Fibres wrap around screens and filter media, choking flow until the pump strains or fails. Fixes usually mean a pump-out and parts replacement — and can add up fast.

3

Lint adds extra solids that don’t break down, so the tank fills faster. That can shorten pumping intervals from years to months — adding recurring pump-out costs.

4

Fine fibres coat the diffuser and restrict airflow, forcing the motor to run hot. The decline is slow and easy to miss until performance drops or backups start.
